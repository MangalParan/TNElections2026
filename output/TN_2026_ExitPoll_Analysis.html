Key Findings & Analysis

📋 Expert Observations — Exit Poll 2026

🏆
DMK Alliance Wins — All Pollsters Agree

Every exit poll released on April 29, 2026 unanimously predicts a DMK-led SPA majority. The midpoint of Poll of Polls gives DMK+ approximately 145 seats — 27 above the 118-majority mark. CM MK Stalin is poised for a second consecutive term.

📉
AIADMK Collapses from Opinion Poll Highs

March 2026 opinion polls (IANS-Matrize) showed AIADMK+ at 114–127 seats — almost matching DMK. Exit polls show a dramatic collapse to 66–100 seats. The TVK factor, leadership vacuum post-Jayalalithaa, and anti-incumbency against EPS appear to have severely dented AIADMK.

⭐
TVK's Remarkable Debut — Spoiler Role Confirmed

Actor Vijay's Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK) is predicted to win 10–26 seats in its very first election with 14–23% vote share. While it cannot form government, TVK has severely cut into AIADMK's vote share — acting as a kingmaker of the opposition's future rather than its own.

🎯
Chanakya vs. Matrize — Widest Divergence

The biggest gap between pollsters is on AIADMK seats: Chanakya gives AIADMK just 50–65 while Matrize gives them 87–100 — a 35-seat difference. This likely reflects differing assumptions about TVK vote-splitting vs. AIADMK consolidation in southern/Kongu belt.

📊
Vote Share vs. Seat Share Paradox

Despite AIADMK getting 31–38% votes (vs. DMK's 36–44%), they're predicted only 29–43% of seats. This is classic First-Past-The-Post distortion — DMK's alliance arithmetic and vote consolidation in multi-party contests gives them a seat bonus of ~10–15% above their vote share.

🗺️
Regional Patterns Expected

DMK expected to dominate Chennai, Vellore, Kancheepuram belts. AIADMK likely holds Kongu Nadu (Coimbatore, Erode) and parts of southern TN. TVK likely wins seats in mixed urban-rural constituencies where Vijay's popularity cuts across caste lines.

🔮
Historical Pattern Holds — Alternation Broken?

Tamil Nadu's alternation pattern (DMK/ADMK swap every 5 years) would have predicted AIADMK winning 2026. However, exit polls suggest the pattern may be broken for the first time since 1984, with DMK winning a second consecutive term — possibly due to TVK splitting the anti-incumbency vote.

⚠️ Disclaimer

Exit polls are post-voting surveys conducted immediately after voters leave booths. They have historically been accurate on the winner in Tamil Nadu (correctly called DMK in 2021) but often miss exact seat counts by ±20–30 seats. The actual results will be announced on May 4, 2026.
