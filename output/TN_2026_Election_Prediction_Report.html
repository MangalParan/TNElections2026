Executive Summary

The DMK-led Secular Progressive Alliance (SPA) is the clear frontrunner to win the 2026 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election, with an ensemble prediction of ~149 seats (range: 132–166) and a 65% win probability. The ADMK+ (NDA) alliance is projected at ~73 seats (range: 56–89) with a 30% win probability. TVK (Vijay) is expected to win ~8 seats but will drain 12–15% of votes, primarily hurting the ADMK+.

Despite Tamil Nadu's iron-clad anti-incumbency tradition — DMK has never won consecutive Assembly elections — unprecedented conditions (ADMK organizational collapse post-Jayalalithaa, TVK vote-splitting, DMK's 2024 Lok Sabha clean sweep of 39/39 seats) make this election a likely exception.

5. Critical Risk Factors

  1. TVK Vote Share Uncertainty — No electoral track record. Could be 5% or 20%. This single variable could swing 30+ seats between SPA and ADMK+.
  2. ADMK-BJP Transfer Efficiency — First Assembly election together. TN has historically rejected BJP. Hindutva may not transfer to Dravidian voters.
  3. Poll Divergence — Polls range from a tight race (IANS-Matrize: ADMK 120) to DMK landslide (Lokpal: DMK 185). Widest divergence ever in TN polling.
  4. Late-Breaking Events — Campaign momentum, communal/caste incidents, central government decisions could shift things in the final weeks.
  5. Sasikala/PMK(R) Spoiler Effect — Could drain just enough ADMK votes in 15-20 crucial seats to tip them to SPA.

6. Why DMK May Break the Anti-Incumbency Pattern

  1. No charismatic opposition leader — Post-Jayalalithaa ADMK lacks the star power that drove alternation
  2. TVK fragmenting the opposition — In every past alternation, the opposition was a united binary force
  3. 2024 validation — No ruling party in TN's history swept all LS seats 2 years before Assembly elections
  4. Welfare delivery — Tangible schemes (breakfast, housing, insurance) building direct beneficiary loyalty
  5. Alliance expansion — DMDK + OPS additions give DMK access to new vote banks that ADMK lost
